How to pronounce Raku

cs-term community-consensus

RAH koo /ˈrɑː.kuː/ mp3
or: RAK oo /ˈræk.uː/

Raku is most commonly pronounced "RAH koo" (/ˈrɑː.kuː/). This is the widely-used reading among engineers, though edge cases exist.

The language formerly known as Perl 6, renamed Raku in Oct 2019 with Larry Wall's blessing for the name. Community pronunciation is RAH-koo — first syllable 'rah' as in cheering 'rah-rah', second 'koo' as in 'cuckoo', stress on RAH. The name honors the Rakudo compiler, a contraction of Japanese Rakuda-dō ('Way of the Camel'), and evokes 楽 raku ('comfort/enjoyment'). English speakers frequently flatten it to RAK-oo (rhyming with 'rack you'), the common Anglicization. Do NOT read it as 'rake-oo'. Note: the cited Hackster.io article covers the rename and etymology but does not itself document the pronunciation.

Alternate readings you might hear:

  • "RAK oo" /ˈræk.uː/

Source: Hackster.io — Perl 6 renamed to Raku (2019)
