How to pronounce Kuma

project community-consensus

KOO mah /ˈkuːmə/ mp3
or: KYOO mah /ˈkjuːmə/

Kuma is most commonly pronounced "KOO mah" (/ˈkuːmə/). This is the widely-used reading among engineers, though edge cases exist.

Envoy-based service mesh originally created and donated by Kong, accepted into the CNCF (now a Sandbox project; repo kumahq/kuma, branded with the bear emoji 🐻). The name is the Japanese word for 'bear' (くま / 熊), so the intended pronunciation follows Japanese: KOO-mah — stress on the FIRST syllable, 'oo' as in 'boot'. English speakers who don't know the origin reach for KYOO-mah (rhyming with 'puma'), which is the common mispronunciation. Standard Japanese pronunciation of くま is KOO-mah.

Alternate readings you might hear:

  • "KYOO mah" /ˈkjuːmə/

Source: Introducing Kuma, the Service Mesh From Kong (Cloud Native Now)

Pronouncing project and product names correctly avoids the small but persistent friction of being gently corrected during standups, conference Q&As, and team calls. Hearing the word a few times locks in the right reading better than reading IPA ever will.
