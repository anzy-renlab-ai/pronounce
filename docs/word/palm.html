How to pronounce PaLM

product contested

pom /pɑːm/ mp3
or: P A L M /ˌpiː eɪ ɛl ˈɛm/

PaLM is most commonly pronounced "pom" (/pɑːm/). This is one of several readings in active use; the developer community hasn't converged on a single answer.

Google brand: "pom" (palm tree); letter-by-letter also heard. Pathways Language Model.

Alternate readings you might hear:

  • "P A L M" /ˌpiː eɪ ɛl ˈɛm/

Source: PaLM paper

Pronouncing project and product names correctly avoids the small but persistent friction of being gently corrected during standups, conference Q&As, and team calls. Hearing the word a few times locks in the right reading better than reading IPA ever will. Pronounce is a community-maintained dictionary — every entry tagged with a confidence level and (where possible) a citable source.

Don't look it up twice — hear it in your editor

Install the Pronounce extension and just hover PaLM — or any of 1640 tech words — to hear it, with IPA and the cited source, without leaving your code.

Install for VS Code Open VSX also Cursor · Windsurf · VSCodium · Zed

Hear it from the command line

$ say-it PaLM # primary × 3 + audible "or: …" for each alternate
$ say-it --alt PaLM # focus on the first alternate
$ say-it --all PaLM # primary AND every alternate, each repeated
$ say-it --solo PaLM # primary only, no "or: …" tail
$ say-it --why PaLM # print the dict entry with source URL
