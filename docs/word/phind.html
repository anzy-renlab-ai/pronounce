How to pronounce Phind

product community-consensus

find /faɪnd/ mp3

Phind is most commonly pronounced "find" (/faɪnd/). This is the widely-used reading among engineers, though edge cases exist.

Phind was an AI search engine and coding assistant built for developers (YC S22, founded by Michael Royzen and Justin Wei; the product went inactive in early 2026). The name is a play on "find," with "Ph" taking the F sound as in "phone" — a single stressed syllable, FIND (/faɪnd/), a homophone of the word "find." Non-native devs sometimes misread the unusual "Ph"-before-consonant spelling as "P-hind" or split it into two syllables; it is simply pronounced identically to "find." Corroborated by a Latent Space podcast where founder Michael Royzen's spoken "Phind" is transcribed as "Find." The pronunciation is universally understood but not explicitly stated in any official source, hence community-consensus.
